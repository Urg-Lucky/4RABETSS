Types of Sports Betting

Nowadays, you can easily find a vast amount of activities where you can place your bets. Here are the types of bets you can place:

Win Bets and Moneyline Bets

Win and moneyline bets are interchangeable. Although said differently, both mean the same thing. One thing may be called over the other depending on where you live.

Most people refer to this bet as a money line wager. Bettors in the United States mostly prefer this term. The same kind of wager is called a win bet in the rest of the globe.

This type of bet is the most basic one, no matter what you call it. With this type of wager, you just simply have to select your bet on who‚Äôs going to win the game you bet on.

For instance, you bet on an upcoming NBA game between the Golden State Warriors and the Los Angeles Lakers.

To place a bet, you need to determine which team you think will win. If you believe that the Lakers will win the game, then you must place a win bet on the Lakers. If the Lakers win the game, you‚Äôll win your wager.

Totals and Over or Under Bets

This type of wager has two interchangeable names associated with it. People in the United States often refer to this bet as total. Meanwhile, the rest of the world typically calls it an over or under bet.

Again, both refer to the same type of sports bet regardless of the name.

A totals bet is another simple wager that‚Äôs great for beginners. With a totals bet, the sportsbook issues a specified total associated with a sporting event.

Oftentimes, the total gets tied to the number of points scored in the game you bet on. You have to decide if you think the total will come in over or under the number set by the sportsbook when placing this type of wager.

For example, if we go back to the same game between the Los Angeles Lakers and Golden State Warriors, a total line of 199 may be set on a sportsbook. You have to bet on over if you believe that both teams will earn a combined score of more than 199 points in the game.

On the contrary, if you feel like the teams will have a combined score of less than 199, you have to bet the under. You will win your totals bet if you pick correctly.

These wager types have the same main idea behind them. Through a sportsbook, bettors will determine which team they think will win the match.

Once done, bettors will assign a total number of points. These points are what they believe the team will acquire to win the game. The bettors will decide if the sportsbook pick will go over or under.

For instance, let‚Äôs again consider a game between the Los Angeles Lakers and Golden State Warriors. If a sportsbook posts a point spread of 5 points in favor of the Lakers, that means the sportsbook expects the Lakers to win by the number of points they posted.

Then, you‚Äôll have to choose between the sportsbook being high or low when placing a point spread wager on this game.

If you think that the Lakers will win the game by at least five points or more, then you have to bet on the Lakers.

However, if you believe that the Warriors will either win or lose the game by 4 points or less, then you have to bet on the Warriors.

How Online Betting Odds Work

It's important to know how odds work. Knowing how to read sports betting lines is also essential. This is because online betting sites often display odds differently, depending on the sport and the location from where you're betting.

Here are three different ways online gambling odds can be displayed:

American Odds

American odds are also known as US odds or moneyline odds. These types of odds are the default online betting odds used by American sportsbooks. US odds are centered around winning or wagering $100 on a given bet. The odds are represented by a plus (+) and minus (-) sign indicating the favorite and the underdog.

Reading this type of odds is easy. Odds accompanied by a minus (-) sign are for favorites.

Take -300 as an example. This means that in order to win $100, you must bet $300.

Meanwhile, odds accompanied by a plus (+) sign are for underdogs. Odds of +300, means you can win $300 for every $100 you stake.

Fractional Odds

Fractional odds are also called British odds, UK odds, or traditional odds. These online betting odds are often used in horse racing.

This type of online gambling odds quotes the net total. It will then be paid out to the bettor if they win. It also factors in their stake.

For instance, a bettor placing a bet on Manchester United with odds of 1/5 can expect a payout of just $1 for every $5 bet made. For someone betting $5, one can expect a total payout of $6.

Decimal Odds

This type of online gambling odds is most popular in Europe, Australia, and Canada.

A lot of people think it‚Äôs the easiest format to understand. Almost all online sportsbooks offer an option to display odds in this format.

Under the decimal odds, the figure quoted is the exact amount that will be paid out if the bet is a winner.

The decimal odds number represents the amount you will win for every $1 you bet. For decimal odds, the number represents the total payout, rather than the profit.
